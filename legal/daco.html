Deathcraft Allowed Client Options (DACO)

Allowed Clients and Mods

Deathcraft has specific guidelines on what types of clients and mods are allowed to ensure fair gameplay for everyone. Please review the following lists to understand what is permitted and what is not.

1. Allowed Clients

Clients that are allowed in their original, unmodified state:

  • Vanilla Minecraft Client
  • OptiFine (original, unmodified)
  • Badlion Client (original, unmodified)
  • Lunar Client (original, unmodified)
  • Laby Mod (original, unmodified)
  • Noiskk Client (original, unmodified)
  • Feather Client (original, unmodified)

2. Allowed Mods

The following mods are allowed when used with either the Fabric or Forge modding frameworks. Make sure they are part of a complete modpack and properly configured:

  • JourneyMap (for map exploration)
  • Xaero's Minimap (for map exploration)
  • Just Enough Items (JEI) (for crafting recipes)
  • AppleSkin (for food saturation display)
  • Sodium (performance and graphical improvements)
  • Iris (graphical improvements and shaders)

3. Prohibited Mods

The following mods and modifications are strictly prohibited as they provide unfair advantages or alter gameplay in ways that are not allowed:

  • Hacked Clients (any modified client used for cheating)
  • Duplication Mods (mods that allow duplicating items)
  • Auto-clickers and Auto-aim Mods
  • Any mod that alters game mechanics in an unfair way (e.g., fly mods, speed mods)
  • X-ray Mods (any mod that allows players to see through blocks)
  • Auto-farming Mods (any mod that automates farming or resource collection)

If you are found using prohibited mods or engaging in actions that violate the mod rules, the following consequences may apply:

  • Warning
  • Temporary Ban
  • Permanent Ban (in cases of serious violations)
